<p>Jeremy goes over the parameters involved in a typical CNN layer, including the kernel size, stride, padding, kernel depth and bias. He presents the arithmetic of the convolution operation as well as various padding strategies that might be used. He also introduces pooling and dilation, useful operations that can be used for downsampling images. These can be useful to reduce the computational complexity of a CNN.</p>
